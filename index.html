Strategy, scope & assumptions

This system is designed to create a qualified, repeatable pipeline of investor, founder, and company conversations across the Netherlands, UK, and US. It is not a guarantee of funding, an acquisition, or a closed transaction. Its job is to identify relevant people, reach them with timely context, and create qualified introductions for the client to progress.

Qualified contact: matches the agreed investor, seller, or founder profile; has a verified work email or approved alternative route; is deduplicated against the client’s CRM and exclusion list; has not been contacted within the agreed cooling-off period; and, where Signals OS is selected, has a relevant approved outreach signal.

Addressable market: approximately 1,500–3,000 qualified contacts across Netherlands and UK technology and agriculture businesses. The US has a materially larger pool, but is treated as a secondary expansion market because Netherlands and UK are the current priority. Outreach is paced and refreshed rather than exhausting the market immediately.

1. Cold email infrastructure

Choose an API- and webhook-capable sending platform. Each client uses their own account, domains, and inboxes, so the sending setup is not shared with another client. The selected platform is also used by Signals OS when included; it is not charged again.

Select one sending platform

Inboxes are Outlook as the primary sending account. Google has tightened cold outreach enforcement significantly since 2024 and is more aggressive on spam classification. Outlook is the primary sending channel for this setup because it provides the capacity needed for this workflow and is less restrictive here than the Google-based alternative. Inboxes connect via OAuth, not SMTP. SMTP-connected inboxes hit a hard protocol throttle of around 5 emails per inbox per day. OAuth Outlook supports 15 per day — at three emails per contact, that's the headroom required.

Domains registered via Porkbun at €10/domain/year. 2-week warmup minimum before first send. API access is required for the selected outreach platform in every configuration. Signals OS uses that existing API connection; it does not create a second platform charge.

2. Lead sourcing

All costs below are per 1,000 contacts found — not per email sent. A contact is found once; this cost does not multiply with follow-up emails.

Apify + LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Sales Navigator gives the most granular targeting available: seniority, function, geography, company financials, and recent activity signals. We run an Apify actor against your Sales Navigator session to export results at scale without per-seat export limits. Requires an active Sales Navigator account behind it. Best for finding specific investor profiles and founders by current role, sector, and location.

Apify + Crunchbase

The strongest dataset for funding history and investor entity data across NL, UK, and US. Tracks deal activity, funding rounds, and named investors in one place. We scrape it via an Apify actor against a Crunchbase Basic API subscription. Best for identifying active investment firms and tracking companies approaching a next-round or exit timeline.

Bespoke Companies House scraper Custom build

Companies House is the UK public company registry, available via a free API. A bespoke scraper pulls director age, company age, SIC codes, and filing patterns to identify UK-incorporated businesses and investment vehicles that fit the exit or seller profile. No ongoing subscription — the data is entirely free. UK targets only.

Bespoke news signal scraper Custom build

Monitors NL and UK business press — including Dutch-language sources — for exit-intent signals: retirement announcements, succession planning coverage, and sector consolidation stories. Each article is processed by an AI model that classifies signal strength and extracts relevant company and contact details. Deployed on a Hetzner VPS at a flat €9/month running cost regardless of volume processed. This cost is not normalised to a per-contact rate — it is a fixed monthly overhead for the signal feed.

One honest note on this method: genuinely off-market deals rarely appear in press. What the scraper surfaces is early-stage exit intent — owners who have started talking publicly about transition, not owners who have already quietly engaged a broker. Lower hit rate than database sources, but it catches contacts who have not yet been approached.

6. Cold Email Marketing Operations Optional retainer

Cold email is not a set-and-forget channel. The infrastructure can stay in place, but buyer behaviour, inbox filtering, spam classification, and the copy patterns that work change over time. A campaign that performed well at launch can gradually lose replies or inbox placement if it is left unchanged.

The strategy changes based on real campaign signals rather than assumptions. For example, in Q2 2025, longer hyper-personalized emails often performed well because they appeared individually researched. By Q3, many buyers had become familiar with AI-style personalization, and shorter emails with lighter, more natural personalization often performed better. By Q1 2026, email providers had implemented more AI-driven inbox filtering. Messages needed to remain relevant and personalized enough to feel legitimate, while still reading like a direct human email rather than a polished marketing sequence, or they risked landing in the marketing section instead of the main inbox.

5. Contact volume

Addressable market assumption: the practical addressable market is estimated at 1,500–3,000 qualified contacts across the Netherlands, UK, and US. The calculator starts at 1,000 contacts per month so outreach can be paced, warmed, and refreshed rather than exhausting the market immediately.

Sending is signal-driven, not scheduled. An email sequence starts when a relevant signal is detected for a contact. Each contact receives three touchpoints — one initial email and two follow-up emails. Contacts are not re-entered into a sequence sooner than once per quarter.
